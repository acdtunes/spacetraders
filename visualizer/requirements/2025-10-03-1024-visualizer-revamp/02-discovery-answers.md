# Discovery Answers

**Date:** 2025-10-03
**Phase:** Discovery

## Q1: Should the visualizer track historical data (ship movements, cargo changes, profits) over time?
**Answer:** No

The visualizer will remain focused on real-time monitoring without historical data persistence.

---

## Q2: Should users be able to send commands to ships (navigate, dock, trade) directly from the visualizer?
**Answer:** No

The visualizer will remain a monitoring/visualization tool, not a command interface. Users will continue to use other tools for ship control.

---

## Q3: Should the visualizer display market data and trade opportunities across systems?
**Answer:** Yes

The visualizer will integrate market data to show:
- Prices at different waypoints
- Trade opportunities and arbitrage possibilities
- Supply/demand information
- Profitable route suggestions

---

## Q4: Should multiple star systems be visible simultaneously in a galaxy/sector view?
**Answer:** Yes

The visualizer will add a galaxy/sector view mode that shows:
- Multiple systems simultaneously
- Fleet distribution across systems
- Jump gate connections
- Strategic overview of operations

---

## Q5: Should the visualizer provide notifications/alerts for important events (low fuel, cargo full, arrival, market opportunities)?
**Answer:** Yes

The visualizer will implement an alert system for:
- Low fuel warnings
- Cargo capacity alerts
- Ship arrival notifications
- Market opportunity alerts
- Configurable thresholds

---

## Summary

**Accepted Features:**
- Market data and trade opportunity visualization
- Multi-system galaxy/sector view
- Notifications and alerts system

**Rejected Features:**
- Historical data tracking
- Ship command controls
